International Conference for Students in Science and Innovation (ISSI)

Organized by The Institute for the Promotion of Teaching Science and Technology (IPST), the International Conference for Students in Science and Innovation (ISSI) is an annual conference for students to share science and innovation projects and connect with other students in Thailand.

To register, students must submit their project’s abstract and paper along with a 1,000 Baht registration fee. Then, the ISSI Committee will consider submitted abstracts and announce the list of students qualified to participate in ISSI. The conference is hosted in July and features keynote sessions with international experts and presentations of student science and innovation projects.

Note: To participate, you must have already completed your project.

  • Deadline: May 31
  • Fee: 1,000 Baht

Tips

  • ISSI is a great option if you’ve already finished a research project and you’re looking to gain more acclaim without doing more work.

  • When deciding whether to register for ISSI, remember that abstracts, poster presentations, and oral presentations are all required to be in English. If you’re a native English speaker, you’ll be a lot more comfortable participating in ISSI compared to other local conferences.

  • If you have previously published your project in a journal (or are planning to do so), avoid plagiarism by letting ISSI know that you do not grant permission to publish your abstract and paper.

  • ISSI tends to be less competitive and encourages students from opposing teams to ask questions and share thoughts to one another. They are also very lenient when giving out awards, so nearly all teams receive medals! The top 5 teams will have their papers published by ISSI as well.
