Young Scientist Competition (YSC) Thailand

YSC Thailand is an annual competition founded by Thailand's National Electronics and Computer Technology Center (NECTEC) and The Science Society of Thailand, with thousands of high school students from all regions of Thailand participating annually. To win the competition, participants must pass three rounds: preliminary, semifinal (regional), and final (national).

Teams create research projects in one of several subject areas: Biology, Chemistry, Computer Science, Engineering, Environmental Science, Mathematics, Material Science, and Physics. Teams will pass the preliminary and regional rounds by competing with other teams in the same subject area. Those that pass both rounds compete in the final round against all other teams to be YSC Winners.

Winners of YSC Thailand constitute less than 3% of registered participants and qualify to compete in the prestigious Regeneron ISEF Competition in the United States.

Tips

- Since YSC and JSTP occur around the same time, many students strategically use the same project in both competitions.

- An important part of YSC is science communication. Be prepared to make multiple videos and posters showcasing your project, and practice presenting your project to an audience. It is a good idea to have an art person on board

- To impress judges, begin with an innovative and creative project idea. When the competition gets increasingly selective, all teams are sure to have great execution of their projects. Having a stellar idea leaves a great impression and will help you stand out from the rest.
