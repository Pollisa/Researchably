Junior Science Talent Project (JSTP)

JSTP was initiated in 1997 by the National Science and Technology Development Agency (NSTDA) where selected middle and high school students from all over the country conduct their own projects for one full year.

JSTP is a great and well-known program to apply for those who want the full research experience but have no prior knowledge. It allows students to conduct research from starting out with an idea to writing a complete report. They provide financial support and match students with college professors to provide advice and mentorship. Throughout the year, JSTP hosts camps around June, November, and April for students to present updates on their project, connect with other student researchers, and learn from experts. After the final presentation, JSTP gives a small number of students scholarships until the PhD level.

To register, students must submit an application of various short and long essay questions as well as a project proposal by December and be interviewed in February for middle school students and June for high school students. High school applicants are categorized into different tracks: Mathematics, Chemistry, Medical Science, Physics, Computer, Engineering, and Agricultural Science. After attending the first camp, participants begin their research projects and work continuously throughout the whole year.

Tips:

- There is no need to have the most sophisticated or complex project. For JSTP, the goal is stressed on the learning process. The initiativeâ€™s main goal is to encourage future scientists to learn more about the research world.

- Since it is asynchronous, students are required to manage their own time. Most students fill their summer working on their JSTP research project.

- JSTP is more of a close-knit community where all mentors and students learn together and help one another. Making friends at the camps is one of the most valuable take-aways from JSTP as they share the same interests as you!

- The JSTP Facebook page and admins are very active and post helpful information regularly. Feel free to ask questions regarding the competition by calling the attached phone number or messaging the page.

- If you would like to, you can speak English in your project presentations. The judges understand!
