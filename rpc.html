Ramathibodi Pitching Challenge (RPC)

The Ramathibodi Pitching Challenge was founded by Rama medical students in hopes of creating a platform where high school students can present their ideas in healthcare management or healthcare innovation.

The competition is organized during March through June. Students in grades 10-12 (year 11-13) compete in teams of 1-3. There are two main categories: Healthcare Management and Healthcare Innovation. After registration, participants submit a full report, in either Thai or English, and a three-minute video to pitch their projects in the final round.

  • Deadline: April 30th
  • Fee: Free
    • - In grade 10-12 or year 11-13
    - Currently studying in Thailand
    - No nationality specifications
    - Ages 20 and under
    - Must register with 1 advisor from school

Tips

  • Plan your research idea early in the year and choose teammates wisely. A good timeline is to gather team members in January, draw up research project ideas in February, find a lab in March, and order necessary equipment and/or bacteria cultures by April. Then, you’ll be ready to work on the wet lab during the summer.

  • To impress judges, begin with an innovative and creative project idea. When the competition gets increasingly selective, all teams are sure to have great execution of their projects. Having a stellar idea leaves a great impression and will help you stand out from the rest.

  • Complete iGEM’s requirements for collaboration, deliverables, and human practices as you go to avoid cramming near deadlines.

  • Don’t make up your team of rising seniors only! The Giant Jamboree in Boston takes place around the BMAT test date and the US early application due date.

  • It is a good idea to have members specializing in different areas, such as having a computer person code the wiki, an art person create the posters, and an event person lead public outreach.

  • iGEM allows you to edit your team roster up until September (2021). This means that you can add and remove team members according to circumstances and the project’s needs.

  • Beware of accidental plagiarism, because iGEM has serious consequences against plagiarism including potential disqualification, monetary repercussions, and participatory ban. Avoid this by attributing the owner of the work you used with citations, acknowledgements, or warm thanks.

  • If you need ideas for your project, all past iGEM projects are uploaded to the website.
